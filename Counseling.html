Counseling

LGBTQIA counseling refers to therapy or counseling that is specifically tailored to the needs of individuals who identify as lesbian, gay, bisexual, transgender, queer, intersex, and/or asexual (LGBTQIA). The goals of LGBTQIA+ counseling can vary depending on the individual and their specific needs, but some common goals include:

  • Providing a safe and non-judgmental space for individuals to explore and express their identities, feelings, and experiences.
  • Helping individuals cope with the unique challenges that can come with being a member of the LGBTQIA community, such as discrimination, stigma, and marginalization.
  • Assisting individuals in understanding and accepting their sexual and/or gender identity.
  • Supporting individuals in navigating the coming-out process and building a supportive community.
  • Helping individuals to improve their self-esteem, self-acceptance and self-love, and overall mental health and well-being.
  • Working with couples and families to address issues related to sexual orientation and/or gender identity and improve relationships.
  • Assist with identity documentation changes and navigating the healthcare system.
  • Addressing specific issues related to trauma, like violence and harassment, and other related mental health concerns.
