There are several important questions to ask before starting LGBT counseling, including:

  • What is the purpose of the counseling? Is it to address a specific issue or concern, or is it for general support and guidance?
  • What is the counselor's experience and expertise in working with LGBT individuals?
  • Is the counselor culturally competent and knowledgeable about the unique challenges and experiences of the LGBT community?
  • Is the counselor supportive of the LGBT community and committed to providing non-judgmental, affirming care?
  • Does the counselor use evidence-based approaches and techniques in their work with LGBT clients?
  • Is the counselor licensed and insured, and do they adhere to ethical standards of practice in their work with clients?
  • What are the counselor's fees and payment policies? Are they affordable and feasible for your budget?
  • What are the counselor's availability and scheduling policies? Can they accommodate your schedule and needs?
  • What is the counselor's cancellation policy? Can you reschedule or cancel sessions if necessary?
